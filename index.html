    About Me

    As a Ph.D student under the direction of David Liberles (previously at the University of Wyoming, now at Temple University) my graduate work focused on the evolutionary dynamics at both the phylogenetic and population genetic level of gene duplicates, a key mechanism in how novel functions arise and how species diverge.

    However, the arrival of novel functionality is the exception across evolutionary history. Many genes retain their ancestral functions over extended evolutionary periods.

    Currently, my postdoctoral research in Claus Wilke's group at UT Austin focuses on the converse question from my graduate work by seeking to understand the constraints which cause genes not to functionally diverge, but to maintain their ancestral function.

    In my spare time I play an absurd amount of Xbox and I'm keen on cheesy monster movies.
